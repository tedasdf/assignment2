Job vacancies, states and territories

The first visualization presents a line chart illustrating job vacancies across various Australian states and territories over time, specifically from August 2019 to August 2024. Each line represents a different state, including New South Wales (NSW), Victoria (Vic), Queensland (Qld), South Australia (SA), Western Australia (WA), Tasmania (Tas), Northern Territory (NT), and the Australian Capital Territory (ACT). The x-axis displays the quarters, while the y-axis quantifies job vacancies in thousands. This visualization effectively highlights trends in job vacancies, allowing for comparisons between states and territories as well as insights into fluctuations over the specified time frame. The interactive tooltip feature enhances the user experience by providing detailed information on vacancies for each state at specific quarters.

Change in Job Vacancies

This dataset provides quarterly changes in job vacancies across various Australian states and territories from May 2023 to August 2024. Each entry lists the percentage change in job vacancies for New South Wales (NSW), Victoria (Vic), Queensland (Qld), South Australia (SA), Western Australia (WA), Tasmania (Tas), Northern Territory (NT), and the Australian Capital Territory (ACT), as well as the overall change for Australia. The data reveals fluctuations in job vacancies, with both increases and decreases observed in different states over the specified quarters. For example, while South Australia saw an increase of 8.7% in May 2023, New South Wales experienced a decline of 11.7%. The dataset also includes seasonally adjusted figures and trend values, which help to smooth out seasonal variations and provide a clearer picture of underlying trends in job vacancies.
